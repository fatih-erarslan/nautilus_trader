## 1. Computational Neuroscience & Biophysics

### 1.1 C. elegans Whole-Organism Simulation

**Relevant Crates:** `sibernetic-hyperphysics`, `hyperphysics-connectome`, `hyperphysics-sph`, `hyperphysics-embodiment`, `hyperphysics-stdp`

The HyperPhysics stack provides a complete computational model of the nematode *Caenorhabditis elegans*, integrating:
- **302-neuron connectome** with 6 biophysical model levels (A→D1)
- **PCISPH fluid dynamics** for soft body simulation
- **96 body wall muscles** with neural-muscle coupling
- **Spike-timing dependent plasticity** for online learning

#### Scientific Foundation

The c302 framework enables multiscale modeling from abstract leaky integrate-and-fire neurons to full Hodgkin-Huxley biophysics with ion channel dynamics:

> "c302 has been developed which aims to facilitate the creation of models of the nervous system and musculature of C. elegans. Models generated by the framework can contain all known cells or subsets thereof, and can have varying levels of biophysical detail."
> — Gleeson et al. (2018)

**Recent Advances (2024):**

- **BAAIWorm** achieved closed-loop brain-body-environment simulation reproducing realistic zigzag chemotaxis (Wang et al., 2024)
- **Biophysical neuron models** for AIY, RIM, AVA interneurons and VA, VB, VD motor neurons (Nicoletti et al., 2024)
- **Deep learning approaches** identified limit cycles underlying pirouette behavior (Chen et al., 2024)

#### Model Level Specifications

| Level | Model Type | Ion Channels | Synapses | Use Case |
|-------|-----------|--------------|----------|----------|
| A | Leaky Integrate-and-Fire | None | Spike-triggered | Fast behavioral studies |
| B | Izhikevich | None | Gap junctions | Network dynamics |
| C | Conductance-based | Basic | Spike-triggered | Detailed neural activity |
| C1 | Conductance-based | Basic | Graded (voltage-dependent) | Analog transmission |
| D | Hodgkin-Huxley | k_slow, k_fast, ca_boyle | Spike-triggered | Full biophysics |
| D1 | Full biophysical | k_slow, k_fast, ca_boyle, leak | GradedSynapse2 (Ca²⁺) | Research-grade |

### 1.2 Spike-Timing Dependent Plasticity (STDP)

**Relevant Crate:** `hyperphysics-stdp`

STDP implements biologically realistic synaptic learning rules where the timing of pre- and post-synaptic spikes determines synaptic weight changes.

#### Scientific Foundation

> "Spike-time-dependent plasticity (STDP) represents an essential learning rule found in biological synapses... Pre-synaptic activity preceding post-synaptic activity may induce long-term potentiation (LTP), whereas the reverse case induces long-term depression (LTD)."

**Neuromorphic Computing Applications:**

Recent research demonstrates STDP implementation on memristive hardware:

- **Pre-conditioned STDP** achieves higher recognition accuracy on MNIST (Zhang et al., 2023)
- **Reward-modulated STDP** enables actor-critic spiking neural networks (Liu et al., 2025)
- **Rate-temporal integration** provides robust learning across spiking frequencies

#### Key References

- Bi, G., & Poo, M. (1998). Synaptic modifications in cultured hippocampal neurons: Dependence on spike timing, synaptic strength, and postsynaptic cell type. *Journal of Neuroscience*, 18(24), 10464-10472.

- Zhang, Y., et al. (2023). A new pre-conditioned STDP rule and its hardware implementation in neuromorphic crossbar array. *Neurocomputing*, 551, 126497.

- Serrano-Gotarredona, T., Masquelier, T., Prodromakis, T., Indiveri, G., & Linares-Barranco, B. (2013). STDP and STDP variations with memristors for spiking neuromorphic learning systems. *Frontiers in Neuroscience*, 7, 2. https://doi.org/10.3389/fnins.2013.00002

## 2. Physics-Inspired Computing

### 2.1 Probabilistic Bits (p-Bits) and Ising Machines

**Relevant Crates:** `hyperphysics-pbit`, `ising-optimizer`

Probabilistic computing with p-bits provides a bridge between classical and quantum computing for solving NP-hard optimization problems.

#### Scientific Foundation

> "As an alternative to scaling transistors for general-purpose computing, the integration of transistors with unconventional technologies has emerged as a promising path for domain-specific computing... p-bits are naturally applicable to combinatorial optimization, such as solving the Boolean satisfiability problem, energy-based generative machine learning models like deep Boltzmann machines, and quantum simulation."
> — Chowdhury et al. (2023)

**Key Results:**

- **Sparse Ising machines** achieve up to 6 orders of magnitude speedup vs. standard Gibbs sampling
- **Stochastic magnetic tunnel junctions (sMTJs)** enable GHz-frequency fluctuations
- **Quantum advantage boundaries** pushed by probabilistic computers on 3D spin glasses (Aadit et al., 2025)

#### Hardware Implementations

| Technology | Speed | Energy | Application |
|-----------|-------|--------|-------------|
| CMOS p-bits | MHz | pJ/flip | FPGA prototyping |
| sMTJ p-bits | GHz | fJ/flip | ASIC production |
| SPAD-based | GHz | Low | Optical computing |

#### Key References

- Camsari, K. Y., Faria, R., Sutton, B. M., & Datta, S. (2017). Stochastic p-bits for invertible logic. *Physical Review X*, 7(3), 031014. https://doi.org/10.1103/PhysRevX.7.031014

- Chowdhury, S., Grimaldi, A., Aadit, N. A., et al. (2023). A full-stack view of probabilistic computing with p-bits: Devices, architectures, and algorithms. *IEEE Journal on Exploratory Solid-State Computational Devices and Circuits*, 9(1), 1-11.

### 2.2 Thermodynamic Computing

**Relevant Crate:** `hyperphysics-thermo`

Thermodynamic computing leverages Landauer's principle to approach fundamental energy limits of computation.

#### Scientific Foundation

> "Landauer's principle states that the minimum energy needed to erase one bit of information is proportional to the temperature: E_min = k_B T ln 2 ≈ 2.87 × 10⁻²¹ J at room temperature."
> — Landauer (1961)

**Recent Advances (2023-2024):**

- **Parallel computing** can maintain near-Landauer efficiency even at finite time (Kolchinsky et al., 2023)
- **Reversible programs** show 6-240% energy overhead depending on abstraction level
- **Watts-per-intelligence** framework links algorithmic thermodynamics to machine intelligence

#### Energy Hierarchy

| Operation | Theoretical Minimum | Current CMOS | Gap |
|-----------|-------------------|--------------|-----|
| Bit erasure | 2.87 × 10⁻²¹ J | ~10⁻¹⁵ J | ~10⁶× |
| Logic gate | ~k_B T ln 2 | ~10⁻¹⁵ J | ~10⁶× |
| Reversible gate | 0 (ideal) | ~10⁻¹⁶ J | N/A |

#### Key References

- Landauer, R. (1961). Irreversibility and heat generation in the computing process. *IBM Journal of Research and Development*, 5(3), 183-191.

- Bennett, C. H. (2003). Notes on Landauer's principle, reversible computation, and Maxwell's Demon. *Studies in History and Philosophy of Science Part B*, 34(3), 501-510.

- Kolchinsky, A., Marvian, I., Gokler, C., et al. (2023). Fundamental energy cost of finite-time parallelizable computing. *Nature Communications*, 14, 444.

### 3.1 Hyperbolic Neural Networks

**Relevant Crates:** `hyperphysics-geometry`, `hyperphysics-lorentz`, `hyperphysics-hnsw`

Hyperbolic geometry (H³, K=-1) provides natural representations for hierarchical and tree-like data structures.

#### Scientific Foundation

> "Poincaré embeddings can learn parsimonious representations of symbolic data by simultaneously capturing hierarchy and similarity... The Lorentz model significantly outperformed the Poincaré model, while Euclidean representations yielded the poorest results."

- **Exponential volume growth**: Trees embed with low distortion
- **Natural hierarchy**: Parent-child relationships preserve distances
- **Efficient capacity**: Fewer dimensions needed for complex graphs

#### Recent Advances (2023-2024)

- **HypStructure** (NeurIPS 2024): Regularization for embedding label hierarchies
- **Hyperbolic embeddings of supervised models** (NeurIPS 2024)
- **Comprehensive survey** on hyperbolic deep learning in computer vision (Mettes et al., 2024)

#### Applications

#### Key References

**Relevant Crate:** `hyper-risk-engine`

Optimal position sizing using Kelly criterion maximizes long-term portfolio growth.

#### Scientific Foundation

> "The Kelly Criterion aims to maximize the logarithm of wealth over time, ensuring optimal growth rate while managing downside risk."
