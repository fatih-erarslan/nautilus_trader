## ðŸŽ­ Part 2: Dual-Process Theory (Kahneman)

### System 1 vs System 2
**System 1 (Fast)**
- Automatic, intuitive, emotional
- Pattern recognition
- <100ms response time
- Handles 95%+ of daily decisions
- Prone to biases but energy-efficient

**System 2 (Slow)**
- Deliberate, logical, effortful
- Complex problem-solving
- Seconds to minutes response
- Only ~3% of decisions
- Accurate but resource-intensive

### Trading Implications
- High-frequency trading = System 1 dominant
- Portfolio rebalancing = System 2 dominant
- Risk management needs both systems
- Biases in System 1 can be exploited or avoided

## ðŸ›ï¸ Part 3: Cognitive Architectures

### SOAR Architecture
- **Problem Space Hypothesis**: All behavior as search through state space
- **Impasse-driven learning**: Automatically creates substates when stuck
- **Chunking**: Compiles solutions for reuse
- Serial bottleneck with parallel rule firing
- ~50ms operator application (matches human reaction time)

### ACT-R Architecture
- **Production System**: Rule-based cognitive processing
- **Dual Memory**: Separate declarative and procedural
- **Activation-based retrieval**: Most relevant memories surface
- **Predictive capabilities**: Anticipates human behavior
- Focus on detailed cognitive modeling

### CLARION Architecture
- **Dual-process integration**: Explicit + implicit reasoning
- **Dynamic learning**: Adapts to changing environments
- **Neural-symbolic hybrid**: Combines rules and networks
- Ideal for human-AI collaboration

### Trading Applications
- SOAR: Good for systematic strategy development
- ACT-R: Excellent for modeling trader behavior
- CLARION: Best for adaptive market conditions

## ðŸŽ¯ Recommended Architecture for CWTS

Based on this research, I recommend a **5-layer hierarchical architecture** that combines:

1. **Reflexive Layer** (System 1, Amygdala-inspired)
   - Parasitic organism reflexes

   - Order book analysis
   - Micro-arbitrage

3. **Strategic Layer** (System 2, PFC-inspired)
   - Regime detection
   - Portfolio optimization
   - Risk allocation

4. **Cognitive Layer** (Learning system, Hippocampus)
   - Pattern recognition (HRM-style)
   - Reinforcement learning
   - Memory consolidation

5. **Meta-Cognitive Layer** (Self-awareness, Meta-PFC)
   - Performance attribution
   - Strategy evolution
   - Hypothesis testing

Each layer should:
- Operate at its natural timescale
- Communicate bidirectionally with adjacent layers
- Have veto power over lower layers
- Learn independently but share insights
- Be monitored by CQGS sentinels
